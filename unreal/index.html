A knack for the cinematic

Because of technology like Unreal Engine 5, video games have become more cinematic than ever. They transition between passive and interactive entertainment so smoothly that you almost forget you’re playing a game. Environments come to life like never before, and character performance is now so natural that you can’t resist being moved by it.

The portfolio piece below is mainly meant to demonstrate my penchant for the cinematic and my proficiency with modern tools like Unreal Engine 5. This piece is also meant to show my narrative design skills in action, by way of the creative sensibilities used to plan the piece and the technical know-how used to implement it.

Technical Design & Implementation

Blueprint Scripting

In this snippet of visual scripting, a single cinematic sequence is being played in segments – one for each time the player presses the right trigger button while engaged with the tongs.

With each button press, the player character climbs farther along the tongs, until finally reaching a position to launch himself.

Level Sequences

This example level sequence controls an instance of the player character, one of the “brother” non-playable characters, and a couple cameras.

A baked animation is used to show the brother walking across the level, while keyframed animations provide a quick visualization of the characters’ conversation.

At the end of the level sequence, the camera blends into the player character’s camera, allowing a smooth transition from cinematic sequence to live gameplay.

This snippet shows how easily baseline interactivity can be prototyped in Unreal using Blueprint scripting.

Narrative Design & Writing

Game Specs

  • Title: Runt
  • Audience: Young adults and up (rated T for Teen)
  • Genre: Action-adventure
  • Platform: Console/PC
  • Style: 3D; third-person; semi-gritty with bright spots.
  • Logline: The runaway runt of a family of alien settlers must uncover his hidden superpowers in order to save everyone from an evil mountain hermit.
  • Theme: It’s okay if you change in the process of finding yourself, because family will always be at your side.

Game Overview

Runt is a fable-like underdog adventure story, where the hero essentially goes from meek puppy to brave guard dog.

You play as the youngest and weakest member of a big family of superhuman-esque beings on a verdant alien homestead, where survival means being able to fight all kinds of vicious creatures.

One day, your siblings’ teasing goes too far, so you run away from home in search of self-worth, purpose, and strength. You cross paths with an evil hermit and realize you must become more powerful than anyone ever imagined in order to save your family and home.

Thankfully, with the help of your trusty pet and a few friends you make along the way, you’re able to overcome your fear and self-doubt and discover the strength within yourself to save the day. Despite whatever differences you may have had with your family, everyone is just happy to have you back home again.

Level 1 Overview

This portfolio piece zooms in on one particular level within the hypothetical Runt game: the very start, which is meant to include a higher concentration of cinematic content in order to endear the characters to the player and to draw the player into the rest of the game.

  • Level Plot: You (Thorn) help your giant siblings prepare for the morning hunt.
  • Character Story: You get teased a lot for being the runt of the family, but it’s never truly malicious, so everyone seems to get along well enough, even though you can’t help but feel hurt by it sometimes.
  • Implied Author: “If there’s one thing to know about Thorn, it’s that what he lacks in size he makes up for in resolve and nobility.”
