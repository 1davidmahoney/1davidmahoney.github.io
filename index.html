Introduction

Story is fundamental to the human experience – whether telling a story, imagining one, or living one to tell later. I’ve been honored to continue this age-old tradition through my work on some incredible properties.

Lone Echo II

As a lifelong sci-fi fan and VR enthusiast, it was a dream come true working on Lone Echo II. Unexpectedly taking this dream a step further, I had the incredible honor of seeing the project win the D.I.C.E. Award for Immersive Reality Game of the Year and be nominated for the Webby Award for Best Narrative Experience.

  • Generator A – Learn about your new companions while restoring power.
  • Astraea Wreckage I & II – Mend relationships while nabbing alien tech.
  • Research Facility – Learn about your companion’s long-lost love interest while upgrading your robot shell.
  • Chiron Station – Relive the hopes and dreams – and horrible fates – of the former station crew while exploring inside and outside the station.

I’m especially proud of the dialogue I wrote for the Research Facility, where you discover the heart-rending message left behind for one of your companions.

Development

The biggest challenge was the "throw" action, which required careful tuning for it to feel good in the large-scale environment. Because your target is always far away – either in the skies above or on the ground below – the projectile must leave the hand more quickly than might be expected. Too slow and realistic, and you feel oddly underpowered. Too quick, and you feel overpowered and out of control.

Next Steps

Although I was able to tune the system to a good middle ground, I'm looking forward to trying other aim-assist methods in future iterations of the prototype.
